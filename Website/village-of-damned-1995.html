Thoughts

I really enjoyed the 1960 original version of this movie, and I expected better of John Carpenter, so what happened? This movie was a mess. It was poorly paced, with almost no rhyme or reason to the structure of the scenes. Some are long and pointless, others are short and purposeful, or some variation of those. The dream sequence warning was stupid and could have been done better, such as: The belly of anyone who was thinking of getting rid of the child could light up and they suddenly decide not to. The acting wasn’t terrible, and David was pretty good. The music was decent, though I’ve never complained about music in any Carpenter movie. The Alien reveal was horrible, and the puppet of the alien was bad. Speaking of the alien, why did the baby turn into an alien thing at all? The other children never looked anything like an alien, so why did that one? The gun fight seemed entirely unnecessary, just padding for a movie that should have had more time devoted to people interacting with the children like the original did, instead of too many scenes of the children being threatening. They acted threatening so often that they stopped being threatening and just became silly. The children in the original were subtle, sinister in the unknown of them, and only used their powers when they were threatened, but in this, they attacked for the slightest annoyance, and it got to be too much.
