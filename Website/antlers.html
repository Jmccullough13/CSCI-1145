Summary

Text states that a malevolent spirit has been awakened to prey on the weak, depraved, and lost because the Earth has been pillaged and stripped of its life blood. A child, Aiden, plays in an abandoned railway station next to a derelict mining facility. He tells the kid not to leave the truck and goes back into a mine, putting a gas mask on. He hears a growl but ignores it and continues down to another man packing up equipment from a drug lab. Thoughts

This movie was very, very heavy, dealing with a lot of physical violence issues and the mental issues that come with them. Julia was abused, Lucas was abused and had to deal with watching his father and brother essentially die. Lucas also had to come to terms with his brother becoming a monster and had to stand by as he was killed to prevent that. The horror aspects were overshadowed by the heavy aspects, so I was left feeling the impacts of those rather than the monster itself. The monster was good, designed by Del Toro himself, but we barely got to see it so focused was the movie on the trauma of the main characters. It had a few brief flashes while attacking and the full fight in the mine at the end, but that was only for a few minutes. We need longer to appreciate Del Toro’s design. The pacing was great throughout the movie, with no scene being too extra or adding anything or didn’t need, but the ending felt somewhat rushed, as the movie was chugging along and then suddenly ended. The performances were fantastic, but I’m never disappointed by Kerri Russel in any movie I’ve seen her in. The kid who played Lucas was especially good, believable in his portrayal of a kid dealing with the trauma of caring for a rapidly transforming father and younger brother. The camera work was great, the music on point. This movie didn’t do as well as I think it should have, but I can see why some people wouldn’t have enjoyed it. I did, and recommend it heartily.
